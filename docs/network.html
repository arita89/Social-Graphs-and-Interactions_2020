The bipartite social network

The network graph consists of 40974 nodes and 95691 edges. The graph is bipartite with 12053 artist nodes on one side, and and 28922 songs nodes on the other. Because the graph is bipartite, edges only connect across sides between artists and songs. As such, an edge represent an artist's participation in a song, or some kind of contribution.

By using projections of each side - the artist side and the song side - the indirect relations between artists respectively the songs are revealed. Essentially, whenever two artist are related to the same song, these two relations are reduced into an edge between the two artists. And likewise with the songs side; songs with artists in common are connected. The end result is two new graphs with a lot more edges/relations than the original graph; one describing co-working relationships between artist, and the other describing how songs relate to each other through the artists who contributed to the song.

The graphs can be visualized via different algorithms. Below ForceAtlas2 is used for positioning the nodes. Whenever possible, these positions are used across visualizations to improve comparability.

The artists projection

The artists projection consists of the 12053 artist nodes, and 109004 relation/edges.

When visualized like this, the artists are grouped together based on the songs they made together.

Not surprisingly, artists tend to group together. Groups located close to each other would to some degree have song relations in common. This could be both the same songs performed by different artists, but also some of the artists taking part in such songs. Artists located between groups would typically have relations to multiple such groups. This could be for instance producers, writers, supporting artists and technicians.

The songs projection

The other side of that bipartite graph, the songs projection, consists of the 28922 songs nodes and 1478291 relations (edges)

Based on the visualization, most songs seem to have enough relations to other songs (via the participating artists) to be pulled towards the center of the 'galaxy'. This indicates widespread connectivity to the same communities of artists.

The songs distant from the center on the other hand have little connection to such large interconnected communities. They might, however, be part of groups of artists not included in the graph. As such, the may in reality be well-connected, but in the scope of this graph, they are not.

Via the bipartite graph it is easily identified how many relations artist has rained thought participation in songs. This is simply done by calculating the degrees of the actor's node in the original bipartite graph.

The primary artists (those used to bootstrap the network) ar much better connected that any of the other roles. They are, however, also part of the the performer group. Hence the other featured artists is apparently not that well connected.

As one could expect, producers also have a handfull of connections. The writers and the custom performance artists draws the shortest straw, generally not having that many relations.

Shortest Paths

Even though the network consists of 12.052 artists, they are so tightly connected, that there is at worst 9 connections between two artists. However, as shown on the histogram, this is much longer that even the worst average shortest path of any artist. I fact, aside for a very small group of artist, the norm is average path lengths between 3.5 and 5.5. The average average path length is 4.2, and to most typical length is 4.0.

This is of cause connections though songs through the year. Therefore, some connections would in reality not be that effective due to natural causes, but this does not matter that much when browsing the music albums, shouting for unknown artists.

Artist degree centrality

Within the artist projection, the node degree describe how many artists an artist has collaborated with. Such collaborations includes even the established relations between the featured artists and technical staff such as audio mechanics.

As expected, the degree of artists vary a lot. However, due to the positioning provided via the ForceAtlas2 algorithm, especially two patterns is noticeable. First, there seem to be some grouping going on between a handful of artist with with high degree. Also, a small group of nodes with high degree seem to act as bridges between isolated groups and the main cloud of artists.

Examination of the different roles, show that the primary artists (the ones used to bootstrap the network) have a higher average degree, which is kind of expected. These primary artists are, however, merged into the role category 'performer', together with other featured artists. The performing artists as a whole (including the primary ones) are actually not that well-connected as could have been initially assumed. These other performing artists are not described in the boxplot, but it is clear when there is such a big difference between the two described roles, these other performing artists must be much less connected than the 'primary' artists. This is also visible below on the plot showing only the performing artists: There are a lot om small red dots - especially in one region of music galaxy.

Compared to the whole network, the producers that have more relations to other artists than the average artist. But this is actually as expected, since many artists use the same producers and some artist fabricate lyrics for performing artists for a living.

Writers stand out as the role that is less connected on average.

In every category there are many outliers: These are the most popular artists.

Artist betweenness centrality

While the artist degree centrality measures the artist's centrality by only its neighbors, betweenness centrality describes how many shortest pats between all artists that pass through an artist. The artists most central artists is then the ones that must paths pass through.

Compared with degree centrality much fewer artists have high centrality. In fact, now only a handful of artist can be said to be considered truly central - large hubs - in the network.

The primary artists mostly, with all featured performers (which still includes the primary performers) and producers following, are the ones who influence the flow around the network. However, there a lot of outliners across all categories. Those artists would be the actual people you would want to know, if you want to get a foot inside the network. For a music enthusiast such artists would, however, possibly just be the big influencers, and someone you would expect everyone to have heard about.

Artist eigenvector centrality

Which nodes have an higher influence over the all network?

Eigenvector centrality evaluates the degree of a node's neighbors, and can therefore be used to better identify nodes with influence over the whole network, and not just those directly connected to it.

With the eigenvector, there are apparently a handful of extreme outliers. Also, the eigenvector centrality of the typical node is very low. In fact close to zero.

Clustering

Does the songs of each artist tend to cluster? That is, do artist tend to work primarily and only with the same artists?

Looking at the role categories, the lowest scoring role is the primary artists (the ones from the original playlist). While at first, this may seem strange, it could for instance be because these artists have simply made their albums with different cliques in the network.

The custom performance artists, have the highest clustering coefficient, but even so, the role still has a 75-percentile close to only 0.45.

Overall the average clustering is quite low. This is somewhat to be expected, since songs are typically not made by the same group of performing artists, producers, writers, singers, technicians etc. However, especially among the custom performance artist, there is actually some artists that have songs that tend to cluster. But these are probably artists that have done very few songs, or are related to only group of artists. There are of cause also artists from the other role categories that have songs that tend to cluster a lot more than the average, but except for the writers, this tend to be outliers.
